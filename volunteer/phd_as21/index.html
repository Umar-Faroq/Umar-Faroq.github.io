Bridging Distributional Discrepancy with Temporal Dynamics for Video Understanding

Slides Poster @ AS

Abstract

Video has become one of the major media in our society, bringing considerable interests in the development of video analysis techniques for various applications. Temporal Dynamic, which represents how information changes along time, is the key component for videos. However, it is still not clear how temporal dynamics benefit video tasks, especially for the cross-domain case, which is close to real-world scenarios. In this talk, I will share my Ph.D. work which effectively exploits temporal dynamics from videos to tackle distributional discrepancy problems for video understanding.
